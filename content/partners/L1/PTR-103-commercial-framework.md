## 1. Per-User, Per-Month Pricing Model

Rainbow uses a subscription pricing model. Every licensed user pays a monthly fee based on the tier assigned to them. There are no per-minute charges for internal calls, no per-meeting charges for conferences, and no per-message charges for instant messaging. Everything is included in the subscription.

### 1.1 How Pricing Works

The fundamental unit is **one user, one month**. The price varies by tier:

| Tier             | Price Range (Indicative)  | Billing Cycle     |
|------------------|---------------------------|--------------------|
| Essential        | Free (EUR 0)              | N/A                |
| Business         | EUR 7 - 12 / user / month | Monthly or Annual  |
| Enterprise       | EUR 14 - 22 / user / month| Monthly or Annual  |
| Rainbow Connect  | EUR 10 - 18 / user / month| Monthly or Annual  |
| Rainbow Hub      | Custom pricing            | Annual             |

**Important notes on pricing:**

- Exact pricing depends on the partner agreement, volume commitments, contract term, and regional pricing schedules. The figures above are indicative ranges to help you understand the relative positioning. Always refer to the current ALE partner price list for binding quotes.
- Annual billing contracts receive a discount compared to monthly billing (typically 10-15% savings).
- The Essential tier is genuinely free with no time limit. There is no trial period. Free means free.

### 1.2 What Is Included

Every paid tier subscription includes:

- Unlimited internal instant messaging and presence.
- Unlimited internal audio and video calls.
- Conference rooms (size varies by tier: Business up to 120 audio / configurable video; Enterprise up to 200+).
- Screen sharing and file sharing.
- Cloud storage allocation for shared files.
- Desktop, web, and mobile client access.
- Standard support via the partner.
- Automatic updates and new feature releases.

What is NOT included (and may be sold as add-ons or through the telephony infrastructure):

- PSTN connectivity (provided by the PBX or an SBC with SIP trunking).
- Call recording storage beyond the standard allocation.
- Premium support SLAs (available as a separate agreement).
- Professional services (deployment, migration, custom integration).

### 1.3 Contract Terms

Rainbow subscriptions are available in two contract terms:

- **Monthly:** No long-term commitment. Higher per-user price. Ideal for proof-of-concept deployments, seasonal workers, or customers who refuse to commit annually.
- **Annual:** Twelve-month commitment with a discount. Users can be added mid-term at the prorated annual rate. Reductions typically take effect at renewal. This is the standard contract for most enterprise deals.

Multi-year contracts (24 or 36 months) may be negotiated for large deals with additional discounting, subject to ALE approval.

## 2. Mix-and-Match Tier Strategy

The ability to assign different tiers to different users within the same organization is one of Rainbow's most powerful commercial weapons. Most competitors require all users to be on the same plan, forcing the customer to pay for the most expensive tier across the board or accept the lowest common denominator of features.

### 2.1 How It Works

Within a single Rainbow company account, the administrator can assign any tier to any user. Users on different tiers communicate seamlessly — an Essential user can message an Enterprise user, join a conference hosted by a Business user, and receive calls routed by the PBX (if the Essential user's role does not require it, they simply do not have the telephony control feature).

### 2.2 Strategic Application

Consider a manufacturing company with 1,000 employees:

| User Group              | Count | Tier        | Monthly Cost (Est.) | Total Monthly   |
|-------------------------|-------|-------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Production floor workers | 600  | Essential   | EUR 0               | EUR 0           |
| Office staff with phones | 250  | Business    | EUR 10              | EUR 2,500       |
| Management & compliance  | 100  | Enterprise  | EUR 18              | EUR 1,800       |
| Sales team (Salesforce)  | 50   | Connect     | EUR 14              | EUR 700         |
| **Total**               | **1,000** |         |                     | **EUR 5,000**   |

**Blended per-user cost: EUR 5.00 / user / month.**

If a competitor forces all 1,000 users onto their equivalent of an Enterprise tier at EUR 20/user/month, the total would be EUR 20,000/month. Rainbow's mix-and-match approach delivers the same outcome at 75% less total cost.

### 2.3 Upsell Path

The mix-and-match strategy also creates a natural upsell path:

1. **Land with Essential:** Get the entire organization on Rainbow for free.
2. **Expand to Business:** Convert users who need telephony integration.
3. **Upsell to Enterprise:** Convert users who need compliance and analytics.
4. **Cross-sell Connect:** Add CRM integration for sales and service teams.

Each conversion generates incremental monthly recurring revenue (MRR) for the partner.

## 3. Deal Registration Process and Partner Margins

### 3.1 Deal Registration

Deal registration is the process by which a partner claims a sales opportunity with ALE to receive pricing protection and avoid channel conflict. Registering a deal is essential — unregistered deals receive standard pricing, while registered deals receive discounted buy prices that increase your margin.

**Steps to register a deal:**

1. **Identify the opportunity.** Confirm the prospect has not already been registered by another partner. Contact your ALE channel manager if unsure.
2. **Submit registration.** Log into the ALE Partner Portal and complete the deal registration form. Required fields include: customer name, contact information, estimated number of users, target tiers, estimated annual contract value (ACV), and expected close date.
3. **Receive confirmation.** ALE reviews the registration (typically within 48 hours). If approved, you receive a registration confirmation with a unique deal ID and a registration validity period (usually 90 days, extendable upon request).
4. **Maintain registration.** Update the deal status regularly in the portal. If the deal does not close within the validity period, request an extension before it expires.

### 3.2 Partner Margin Structure

Partner margins on Rainbow subscriptions follow a tiered structure based on the partner's certification level and the deal specifics.

| Partner Level      | Standard Margin | Registered Deal Margin | Notes                               |
|--------------------|-----------------|------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Authorized         | 15 - 20%        | 20 - 25%               | Entry-level partnership              |
| Business Partner   | 20 - 25%        | 25 - 30%               | Volume and competency requirements   |
| Expert Partner     | 25 - 30%        | 30 - 35%               | Highest tier, strategic alignment    |

**Margin calculation example:**

- Customer pays: EUR 18 / user / month (Enterprise tier).
- Partner buy price (Business Partner, registered deal): EUR 18 x (1 - 0.28) = EUR 12.96 / user / month.
- Partner margin: EUR 5.04 / user / month.
- For 200 Enterprise users over 12 months: EUR 5.04 x 200 x 12 = EUR 12,096 annual margin.

These margins are recurring. As long as the customer remains subscribed, the partner earns margin on every renewal. This is the foundation of a predictable recurring revenue business.

### 3.3 Services Margin

In addition to subscription margins, partners can earn revenue from professional services:

| Service                    | Typical Pricing             | Margin    |
|----------------------------|-----------------------------|-----------|
| Deployment & configuration | Per-day rate (partner sets) | 100%      |
| Migration planning         | Fixed project fee           | 100%      |
| Custom integration         | Per-day rate                | 100%      |
| Training                   | Per-session or per-user     | 100%      |
| Managed services           | Monthly recurring           | 100%      |

Professional services are priced and delivered entirely by the partner. ALE does not take a share. This makes services a high-margin opportunity, especially for complex deployments involving PBX integration, CRM connectors, or sovereign Hub installations.

## 4. Volume Tier Discounts

For larger deals, ALE offers volume-based discounts that reduce the partner's buy price. These discounts stack with deal registration discounts.

### 4.1 Volume Discount Tiers

| User Count         | Additional Discount |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| 1 - 99             | Standard pricing    |
| 100 - 499          | 5% additional       |
| 500 - 999          | 10% additional      |
| 1,000 - 4,999      | 15% additional      |
| 5,000+             | Custom negotiation  |

### 4.2 Applying Volume Discounts

Volume discounts are applied to the partner buy price, not the customer sell price. This means the partner can choose to:

1. **Pass the discount to the customer** to win a competitive bid on price.
2. **Keep the discount** to increase margin.
3. **Split the difference** — reduce the customer price slightly while improving margin.

**Example calculation:**

- Enterprise tier list price: EUR 18 / user / month.
- Partner buy price (Business Partner, registered deal, 30% margin): EUR 12.60.
- Volume discount (500 users, 10% additional): EUR 12.60 x 0.90 = EUR 11.34.
- If partner sells at EUR 16 / user / month: margin = EUR 4.66 per user per month.
- Annual margin for 500 users: EUR 4.66 x 500 x 12 = EUR 27,960.

### 4.3 Enterprise Agreements

For deals exceeding 5,000 users, ALE offers custom Enterprise Agreements with:

- Negotiated per-user pricing.
- Committed volume guarantees (minimum user count).
- Multi-year terms (2-3 years) with price protection.
- Named account management from ALE.
- Co-funded marketing and demand generation.

Contact your ALE channel manager to initiate an Enterprise Agreement negotiation.

## 5. Renewal and Expansion Economics

Subscription businesses live and die on retention and expansion. Understanding these economics is critical for building a sustainable Rainbow practice.

### 5.1 Renewal Revenue

When a customer's annual contract renews, the partner earns the same margin as the initial term (subject to any pricing changes in the ALE price list). There is no renewal commission reduction — the margin is perpetual as long as the customer stays.

**Key renewal metrics to track:**

- **Gross Retention Rate (GRR):** Percentage of revenue retained from existing customers, excluding expansion. Target: 90%+.
- **Logo Retention Rate:** Percentage of customers who renew. Target: 95%+.
- **Days to Renewal:** How far in advance you begin the renewal conversation. Best practice: 90 days.

### 5.2 Expansion Revenue

Expansion revenue comes from three sources:

1. **Seat expansion:** Adding more users as the customer grows or rolls out to additional departments/sites.
2. **Tier upgrades:** Moving users from Essential to Business, Business to Enterprise, or adding Connect licenses.
3. **Add-on services:** Selling additional managed services, integrations, or customizations.

**Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** measures total revenue from existing customers including expansion. An NRR above 110% means your existing customer base is growing even without new logo acquisition. This is the gold standard for subscription businesses.

### 5.3 Churn Prevention

To protect renewal revenue:

- Conduct quarterly business reviews (QBRs) with key accounts.
- Monitor usage metrics (active users, messages sent, calls made). Low usage is an early warning of churn.
- Proactively propose feature adoption to increase stickiness (e.g., if they have Business tier but have not activated CRM integration, propose a Connect upgrade).
- Address support tickets promptly. Unresolved issues erode satisfaction.
- Begin renewal conversations 90 days before contract expiration.

## 6. Structuring Competitive Proposals

### 6.1 Proposal Framework

Every Rainbow proposal should follow this structure:

1. **Executive summary:** One paragraph connecting the prospect's business challenge to Rainbow's solution.
2. **Solution architecture:** Deployment model (Public Cloud, Edge, Hub), tier mix, integration plan.
3. **Pricing summary:** Total monthly and annual cost, broken down by user group and tier.
4. **Total cost of ownership (TCO) comparison:** Rainbow vs. the alternative (status quo, or a competitor). Include both subscription costs and hidden costs the competitor does not mention (SBC hardware for Teams Direct Routing, PBX replacement costs for RingCentral, etc.).
5. **Implementation plan:** Timeline, milestones, and partner services included.
6. **Success metrics:** How the customer will measure ROI (reduced travel costs, improved response times, compliance achievement, etc.).

### 6.2 TCO Comparison Best Practices

When building a TCO comparison, include costs that competitors often omit:

| Cost Category              | Rainbow                        | Typical Competitor             |
|----------------------------|--------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Subscription               | Per-user/month (included)      | Per-user/month                 |
| PBX replacement            | Not required (overlay)         | Often required (rip-and-replace)|
| SBC / gateway hardware     | Not required for ALE PBX       | Required for Direct Routing    |
| Migration downtime         | Minimal (overlay approach)     | Significant (full migration)   |
| Training                   | Similar UX across platforms    | New UX for all users           |
| Sovereign compliance       | Built-in (Edge, Hub)           | Third-party add-ons required   |
| CRM integration            | Included in Connect tier       | Third-party connectors (extra) |

### 6.3 Proposal Pricing Tactics

- **Anchor high, offer value:** Start with the full list price and then show the savings from mix-and-match, volume discounts, and annual commitment. The prospect perceives they are getting a deal.
- **Show blended cost:** Always calculate the blended per-user cost after mix-and-match. This number is almost always lower than the competitor's single-tier price.
- **Include Essential users in the count:** If 200 out of 1,000 users are on the free Essential tier, include them in the total. Saying "1,000 users for EUR 5/user/month" sounds better than "800 users for EUR 6.25/user/month" even though the math is the same.
- **Phase the deal:** If the total upfront commitment is too large for the prospect's budget, propose a phased rollout — start with one department or site and expand over 6-12 months.

## 7. Common Pricing Objections and Responses

### Objection 1: "Microsoft Teams is free with our M365 license."

**Response:** "Teams is included in your M365 subscription for chat and meetings, but telephony is not. Microsoft Phone System costs EUR 7-8/user/month plus Calling Plans or Direct Routing infrastructure. When you add the SBC hardware, SIP trunking, and professional services to make Teams a phone system, the total cost often exceeds Rainbow Business — and you still do not get PBX integration or sovereignty. Let me show you the total cost comparison."

### Objection 2: "Your competitor quoted a lower per-user price."

**Response:** "Are they quoting a single tier for all users? With Rainbow, we can assign the free Essential tier to users who only need messaging, the Business tier to users who need telephony, and the Enterprise tier only where compliance requires it. Let me build a mix-and-match proposal — I am confident the blended cost will be lower than their single-tier approach."

### Objection 3: "We do not want a long-term commitment."

**Response:** "Rainbow is available on monthly terms with no long-term contract. You pay only for the months you use. If you are willing to commit annually, we offer a discount of 10-15%, but the choice is yours. Many customers start monthly to prove the value and then switch to annual at renewal."

### Objection 4: "We cannot justify the cost for users who rarely use the system."

**Response:** "That is exactly why we offer the Essential tier at no cost. Users who only occasionally need to send a message or join a meeting can do so for free. You only pay for users who need advanced features like telephony integration, compliance archiving, or CRM connectivity. This means you are paying only for the value each user actually consumes."

### Objection 5: "We are a public sector organization with strict procurement rules. Your pricing model does not fit our budget cycle."

**Response:** "We understand. Rainbow supports annual billing with a single invoice at the start of the fiscal year, which aligns with public sector budget cycles. We can also structure multi-year agreements with fixed pricing to provide budget certainty. Our sovereign deployment model, Rainbow Hub, is specifically designed for public sector requirements — including procurement through nationally accredited partners."

|----------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Per-User/Month             | Fundamental pricing unit: one user, one month, one tier                        |

## Knowledge Check

**Question 1:** A customer has 500 users. 300 need only basic messaging, 150 need PBX telephony integration, and 50 need compliance archiving. What is the most cost-effective tier assignment?

- A) Enterprise for all 500 users.
- B) Business for all 500 users.
- C) Essential for 300, Business for 150, Enterprise for 50.
- D) Essential for all 500 users.

**Answer:** C — Mix-and-match assigns each user the minimum tier that meets their requirements, minimizing total cost while delivering full functionality where needed.

---

**Question 2:** What is the primary benefit of deal registration for a partner?

- A) It guarantees the customer will buy.
- B) It provides pricing protection and increased margins compared to unregistered deals.
- C) It eliminates the need for a contract.
- D) It gives the partner exclusive access to the customer's network.

**Answer:** B — Deal registration provides a reduced buy price (higher margin) and protects the partner from being undercut by another partner on the same opportunity.

---

**Question 3:** A prospect says, "Teams is free with our M365 license." What is the best response?

- A) "Rainbow is also free."
- B) "Teams chat is included, but telephony requires Microsoft Phone System, Calling Plans or Direct Routing, and SBC hardware — adding up to more than Rainbow's subscription when you include all costs."
- C) "You should cancel M365."
- D) "Free tools are not enterprise-grade."

**Answer:** B — The most effective response acknowledges the M365 bundle but exposes the hidden telephony costs that make a true apples-to-apples comparison favorable to Rainbow.

---

**Question 4:** What does a Net Revenue Retention (NRR) rate above 110% indicate?

- A) The partner is losing customers.
- B) The partner's existing customer base is generating more revenue over time through seat expansion and tier upgrades, even before counting new customer acquisitions.
- C) The partner's pricing is too high.
- D) The partner needs to acquire more new customers.

**Answer:** B — NRR above 110% means existing customers are expanding (more users, higher tiers) faster than any churn, creating organic revenue growth from the installed base.

---

**Question 5:** When presenting a Rainbow proposal, why is it advantageous to include free Essential users in the total user count?

- A) It inflates the contract value.
- B) It makes the blended per-user cost appear lower, which is more competitive against single-tier competitors.
- C) ALE requires it.
- D) Essential users generate commission.

**Answer:** B — Including Essential (free) users in the total lowers the blended per-user cost. Saying "2,500 users at EUR 5/user/month" is more compelling than "700 paid users at EUR 17.86/user/month" in a competitive proposal, even though the total cost is identical.

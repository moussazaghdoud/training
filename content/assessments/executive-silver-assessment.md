## Domain 1: Platform Vision & Market Context

### Question 1

```yaml
id: EXEC-CERT-01-Q01

difficulty: easy

  What category of enterprise software does Rainbow belong to?
options:
  a: Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)
  b: Unified Communications as a Service (UCaaS) and Communication Platform as a Service (CPaaS)
  c: Customer Relationship Management (CRM)
  d: Human Capital Management (HCM)
correct_answer: b
explanation: >
  Rainbow operates in the UCaaS (Unified Communications as a Service) and
  CPaaS (Communication Platform as a Service) market. UCaaS encompasses
  the packaged communication and collaboration tools (messaging, voice,
  video, conferencing), while CPaaS provides APIs and SDKs for embedding
  these capabilities into custom applications. Understanding this market
  positioning is essential for strategic conversations.
```

### Question 8

```yaml
id: EXEC-CERT-01-Q08
type: scenario
difficulty: hard
domain: Security & Compliance
question: >
  Scenario: A CISO at a prospective customer asks: "How does Rainbow
  protect our data differently from US-based providers, given the
  implications of the US CLOUD Act?" What is the correct answer?
options:
  a: "Rainbow uses stronger encryption than US providers."
  b: "Rainbow is hosted in European datacenters operated by ALE, a French company not subject to US jurisdiction. Customer data is governed entirely by European law (GDPR), and ALE cannot be compelled by US authorities to produce customer data under the CLOUD Act, unlike US-headquartered providers whose global data is potentially within CLOUD Act reach."
  c: "The CLOUD Act does not apply to communication platforms."
  d: "Rainbow stores data on users' local devices only, so there is nothing in the cloud to access."
correct_answer: b
explanation: >
  The CLOUD Act (Clarifying Lawful Overseas Use of Data Act) allows US
  authorities to compel US-based companies to produce data regardless of
  where it is physically stored. Because ALE is a French company operating
  European infrastructure, it falls outside US jurisdiction. This is a
  material legal and compliance distinction, not just a technical one,
  and it is the correct response for a CISO concerned about data
  protection and legal exposure.
```

### Question 11

```yaml
id: EXEC-CERT-01-Q11
type: true_false
difficulty: easy
domain: Security & Compliance
question: >
  All communication within Rainbow (messages, voice, video) is encrypted
  in transit using TLS/SRTP protocols.
options:
  a: "True"
  b: "False"
correct_answer: a
explanation: >
  Rainbow encrypts all communication in transit: messaging and signaling
  use TLS (Transport Layer Security) over HTTPS/WSS, and voice/video media
  uses SRTP (Secure Real-time Transport Protocol). This ensures that data
  cannot be intercepted in transit between clients and servers or between
  participants in a call. Encryption in transit is a baseline security
  requirement for enterprise communication platforms.
```

```yaml
  savings: $360K. Net of Rainbow cost ($180K), the annual saving is $180K,
  representing a 100% first-year ROI. This is the type of financial
  narrative that resonates with CFOs.
```

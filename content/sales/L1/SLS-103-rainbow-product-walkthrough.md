## 1. Demo Fundamentals: How to Show Rainbow

![video: Rainbow Sales Demo — Best Practices and Feature Walkthrough](sls-product-demo-walkthrough)

### 1.1 Why the Demo Matters More Than the Slide Deck

In communications platform sales, the product demo is the single most influential moment in the sales cycle. Buyers have seen hundreds of slides about "unified communications" and "seamless collaboration." They have heard every vendor claim to be the best. What they have not done is experience your product firsthand.

A great demo does three things simultaneously:

1. **Proves the product works** — It eliminates the gap between marketing promises and reality
2. **Creates emotional engagement** — The buyer starts imagining their team using the product
3. **Surfaces objections early** — Questions that would have lingered for weeks come out immediately

A poor demo does the opposite: it raises doubts, creates confusion, and gives the buyer an excuse to default to the incumbent (usually Microsoft Teams).

### 1.2 Demo Preparation Checklist

Before every demo, complete this checklist:

- [ ] Rainbow desktop client installed and updated to the latest version
- [ ] Stable internet connection tested (minimum 10 Mbps up/down for video)
- [ ] Demo account populated with realistic contacts, Bubbles, and message history
- [ ] Screen sharing tested on the presentation platform (Zoom, Teams, or native Rainbow)
- [ ] Notifications from other applications silenced
- [ ] Backup plan ready (web client URL bookmarked in case desktop client fails)
- [ ] Browser tabs with admin portal already logged in
- [ ] CRM connector demo environment prepared (if relevant to this prospect)

> **Warning:** Never demo Rainbow on an empty account. A blank contact list and empty Bubbles make the platform look lifeless. Spend 30 minutes before every demo populating your account with realistic data — fictional colleagues, active Bubbles with message threads, shared files, and scheduled meetings.

### 1.3 The Demo Flow: Start with the Story, Not the Feature

Do not launch the demo by clicking through menus. Start by telling a story:

"Let me show you what a typical morning looks like for someone on your team using Rainbow. They arrive at the office, open Rainbow, and instantly see who is available, who is in a meeting, and who is out of office — that is presence. They check their Bubbles for overnight updates — that is team messaging. They have a meeting at 10 AM with a client — they join with one click, and AI automatically transcribes the conversation and generates a summary with action items. All of this happens in a single application."

Then walk through each feature in the context of that story.

## 2. Core Feature Demonstration

### 2.1 Messaging and Presence

**Messaging** is the foundation of daily Rainbow usage. In your demo, show:

- **One-to-one conversations**: Send a message to a contact. Show that messages are delivered instantly with read receipts. Highlight the ability to send text, images, files, and emojis.
- **Message formatting**: Bold, italic, code blocks, and links. Enterprise users care about formatting because they use messaging for professional communication, not just casual chat.
- **Search**: Type a keyword in the search bar and show how quickly Rainbow retrieves historical messages. For organizations drowning in email, searchable messaging is a revelation.
- **File sharing**: Drag a file into a conversation. Show the inline preview for images and PDFs. Mention the storage quotas (which vary by tier).

**Presence** is the feature that buyers underestimate until they see it. Show:

- **Automatic presence states**: Available (green), Busy (red), Away (yellow), Do Not Disturb (red with icon), Offline (grey)
- **Calendar-synced presence**: When a user has a meeting in Outlook or Google Calendar, their presence automatically changes to Busy. This eliminates the guessing game of "Is this person available?"
- **Manual presence override**: Users can manually set their status with a custom message (e.g., "Working from home, available via chat")
- **Presence in the contact list**: Open the contact list and highlight how presence indicators are visible next to every name. "Your team knows at a glance who is available — no more wasted calls to people who are in meetings."

### 2.2 Bubbles (Group Collaboration Spaces)

Bubbles are Rainbow's group collaboration feature. They are similar to channels in Slack or teams/channels in Microsoft Teams, but with some important differences.

**What to demonstrate:**

- **Creating a Bubble**: Click the "+" button, name the Bubble, add members. Show how fast and intuitive the process is. "You can create a project workspace in under 30 seconds."
- **Bubble messaging**: Send a message in the Bubble. Show that all members receive it in real time.
- **File sharing in Bubbles**: Upload a document to the Bubble. Show the shared files panel where all Bubble documents are organized.
- **Bubble membership**: Show how to add and remove members. Highlight that Bubbles can contain up to **300 members** on the Business tier and above.
- **External guests**: Show how to invite external users (customers, partners) into a Bubble. The external user can join with a free Essential account. "You can collaborate with your suppliers and customers in the same workspace, without giving them access to your internal systems."
- **Persistent history**: Scroll up in a Bubble to show historical messages. "Unlike email, everything is in one thread. A new team member joining the project can scroll back and see the full history."

> **Tip:** During a demo, create a Bubble live and name it after the prospect's company. "Let me create a Bubble called 'Project Acme' — this is what your team would see on Day 1." This small act of personalization makes the demo feel real rather than canned.

### 2.3 Voice and Video Calling

Rainbow supports voice and video calling via WebRTC (for cloud-based calls) and PBX integration (for telephony-connected tiers).

**What to demonstrate:**

- **Starting a call**: Click the phone icon next to a contact to start a voice call. Show the call controls: mute, hold, transfer.
- **Upgrading to video**: During a voice call, click the video icon to upgrade to video. Show that the transition is seamless — the call does not drop or reconnect.
- **Call quality indicators**: Point out the network quality indicator during the call. "Rainbow continuously monitors your connection quality and adjusts automatically."
- **Call from any device**: Mention that the user can start a call on their desktop and switch to mobile mid-call (or vice versa). This is especially valuable for users who move between their desk and meeting rooms.

For prospects with PBX environments (Enterprise and Rainbow Connect tiers):

- **Desk phone control**: Show how the Rainbow client can control the desk phone — answer, hang up, transfer, and hold — from the software interface
- **Single number reach**: Explain that incoming calls ring on both the desk phone and the Rainbow client simultaneously. The user answers wherever is most convenient.
- **Call logs**: Show the unified call log that includes both PBX calls and cloud calls in a single view

### 2.4 Conferencing

Conferencing is a high-value feature that directly competes with standalone tools like Zoom and Teams meetings.

**What to demonstrate:**

- **Scheduling a conference**: Open the conference scheduler. Show calendar integration — pick a date and time, add participants, and send invitations.
- **Joining a conference**: Click the join button from the Rainbow client. Show the pre-join screen where users configure their audio and video settings.
- **Participant capacity**: Mention the **120-participant** limit. "For most internal and client meetings, 120 participants is more than sufficient — and it comes included with no additional per-meeting charges."
- **Screen sharing**: Share your screen during the conference. Toggle between full-screen sharing and application-specific sharing. Show the annotation tools if available.
- **Gallery view**: Show the multi-participant video grid. Rainbow supports up to 49 simultaneous video streams in gallery view.
- **Recording**: Start a conference recording. Show where recordings are stored and how they can be accessed after the meeting.
- **Meeting controls for the host**: Mute/unmute participants, lock the meeting, remove participants, promote co-hosts.

### 2.5 Screen Sharing and File Sharing

Screen sharing deserves specific attention because it is one of the features prospects evaluate most critically.

- **Full screen vs. application sharing**: Demonstrate both modes. Application sharing is particularly valuable for users who have confidential information on other windows.
- **Remote control** (if available): Show how a participant can request remote control of the shared screen. This is valuable for IT support scenarios.
- **Performance**: Emphasize the smoothness of screen sharing. Lag or pixelation during a demo will immediately raise concerns about production quality.

File sharing capabilities to highlight:

- **Inline previews**: Share a PDF and show how it renders inline without requiring a download
- **Version history**: If available, show how previously shared versions of a file are preserved
- **Storage quotas**: Mention that storage quotas scale with the subscription tier
- **Security**: Files are encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256)

## 3. AI Features Demonstration

### 3.1 Why AI Is a Must-Show in 2025-2026

Every enterprise buyer is asking about AI. If your demo does not include AI features, you are missing an opportunity to differentiate Rainbow from competitors whose AI capabilities are less mature or not sovereignty-compliant.

Rainbow's AI features are available on the **Enterprise tier** and through the **Rainbow Hub API**.

### 3.2 Live Transcription

**What to demonstrate:**

- Start a conference call (even with just yourself and one other test account)
- Enable live transcription from the meeting controls
- Speak clearly for 30-60 seconds about a business topic
- Show the real-time transcript appearing alongside the video feed
- Highlight the speaker identification: Rainbow attributes each statement to the correct participant

**Sales messaging**: "Your team members who miss a meeting can read the full transcript afterward. No more 'Can you send me the notes?' emails. It is automatic."

### 3.3 Smart Summaries

**What to demonstrate:**

- After a conference call ends, open the meeting summary
- Show the AI-generated summary with three sections: key discussion points, decisions made, and action items
- Highlight that action items are attributed to specific participants

**Sales messaging**: "How much time does your team spend writing meeting notes? With Rainbow, the AI writes the summary for you — including who is responsible for each action item. That is 15-30 minutes saved per meeting, per person."

![diagram: Rainbow AI Meeting Flow — Transcription to Summary to Action Items](sls-ai-meeting-flow)

### 3.4 Real-Time Translation

**What to demonstrate:**

- In a messaging conversation, send a message in one language
- Show the translation option that allows the recipient to view the message in their preferred language
- If possible, demonstrate during a live call where transcription appears in the viewer's language

**Sales messaging**: "For global organizations with teams in multiple countries, real-time translation eliminates language barriers. A team member in Paris can speak French, and a colleague in Munich reads the transcript in German — automatically."

> **Key Concept:** When demonstrating AI features, always connect them to a business outcome, not a technology capability. "AI transcription" is a feature. "Your team saves 30 minutes per meeting and never misses an action item" is an outcome. Buyers buy outcomes.

## 4. Admin Portal Tour

### 4.1 Why the Admin Portal Matters in Sales

You might think the admin portal is only relevant to IT Directors. In reality, it matters to three personas:

- **IT Directors** care about user management, security controls, and deployment simplicity
- **CIOs** care about analytics and organizational visibility
- **CFOs** care about license management and usage data (are we paying for licenses nobody uses?)

A 5-minute admin portal walkthrough in your demo addresses all three audiences simultaneously.

### 4.2 Key Admin Portal Features to Show

**User Management**

- Show the user list: search, filter by department, sort by status
- Demonstrate how to add a new user: "Adding a user takes three clicks. Enter their email, select their tier, and assign them to a group. Done."
- Show bulk user import via CSV: "For large deployments, upload a spreadsheet and provision 1,000 users in minutes."
- Highlight LDAP / Active Directory synchronization (Enterprise tier): "Connect Rainbow to your Active Directory. Users are provisioned and deprovisioned automatically when they join or leave the company. No manual work."

**Analytics Dashboard**

- Show the usage analytics: active users, messages sent, calls made, meetings held
- Highlight trend graphs: "Your IT team can see adoption trends over time. If usage drops, they know to investigate."
- Show the quality metrics: call quality scores, network performance data
- Mention exportable reports: "These reports can be exported for executive presentations or compliance audits."

**Security and Compliance Controls**

- Show the security settings: password policies, two-factor authentication options, IP whitelisting
- Highlight data retention policies: "You control how long messages and files are retained — critical for compliance."
- Show the audit log: "Every admin action is logged and auditable. Your compliance team can review who changed what, and when."

**Deployment Options**

- Show the client download links: Windows (MSI), macOS, iOS, Android
- Mention enterprise deployment: "The Windows client is available as an MSI package that your IT team can deploy via SCCM, Intune, or Group Policy. No user interaction required."
- Highlight the web client: "Users who cannot install software — contractors, guests, shared workstations — access Rainbow through the browser. Nothing to install."

> **Info:** For prospects with more than 500 users, offer to walk the IT Director through the admin portal in a separate, dedicated session. A 30-minute deep-dive on administration features can convert a skeptical IT Director into a Champion.

## 5. Client Platforms and Integrations

### 5.1 Six Client Platforms

Rainbow is available on **six platforms**, ensuring that every user in the organization can access the same communication features regardless of device:

| Platform          | Target Use Case                                          | Deployment Method                     |
|-------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| **Web**           | Guest access, shared workstations, BYOD                  | Browser — no installation required    |
| **Windows**       | Primary desktop client for most enterprise users         | MSI installer, SCCM, Intune, GPO     |
| **macOS**         | Desktop client for macOS users                           | DMG download, MDM deployment          |
| **iOS**           | Mobile access for iPhone and iPad users                  | Apple App Store, MDM distribution     |
| **Android**       | Mobile access for Android phone and tablet users         | Google Play Store, MDM distribution   |
| **Android TV**    | Meeting room and conference room displays                | Google Play Store on Android TV       |

**How to position multi-platform in the sales conversation:**

"Your CEO uses a MacBook. Your warehouse team uses Android phones. Your receptionist uses a Windows desktop. Your conference rooms have a screen on the wall. Rainbow works on all of them, with the same features and the same user experience. One platform, every device."

### 5.2 CRM Connectors

CRM integration is a high-value differentiator for prospects in sales-driven and service-driven organizations. Rainbow offers pre-built connectors for the major CRM and service management platforms:

**Salesforce Connector**

- Click-to-call from Salesforce contact records
- Automatic call logging: every Rainbow call is logged as an activity in Salesforce
- Presence visibility: see a colleague's Rainbow presence status within Salesforce
- Screen pop: when an incoming call arrives, the Salesforce contact record pops up automatically

**ServiceNow Connector**

- Click-to-call from ServiceNow incident and case records
- Call logging and note capture directly within ServiceNow
- Integration with ServiceNow workflows: trigger Rainbow notifications from ServiceNow events

**Microsoft Dynamics 365 Connector**

- Click-to-call from Dynamics contact and account records
- Call activity synchronization between Rainbow and Dynamics
- Presence integration within the Dynamics interface

**SAP Connector**

- Integration with SAP Business ByDesign and SAP S/4HANA
- Click-to-call from SAP contact records
- Activity logging within SAP

**How to position CRM connectors in the sales conversation:**

"Your sales team spends their day in Salesforce. With Rainbow's connector, they click a phone number in Salesforce, the call happens through Rainbow, and the call is automatically logged — no copy-pasting, no forgotten follow-ups. That is 10-15 minutes saved per salesperson per day, and better CRM data quality for your managers."

### 5.3 Calendar and Directory Integrations

Beyond CRM, Rainbow integrates with the tools that enterprise users rely on daily:

- **Microsoft Outlook**: Calendar sync for presence, meeting scheduling from Outlook, email-to-Bubble sharing
- **Google Calendar**: Calendar sync for presence, meeting scheduling
- **Microsoft Active Directory / LDAP**: Automatic user provisioning and deprovisioning, group synchronization
- **Microsoft Teams coexistence**: Rainbow can run alongside Teams. Users do not have to choose one or the other. This is particularly important for prospects who already have Teams deployed and are reluctant to abandon it entirely.

### 5.4 Demo Strategy: Match the Integration to the Buyer

Not every integration matters to every buyer. Use your discovery findings to decide which integrations to demonstrate:

| If the Prospect Uses...         | Demonstrate This Integration              |
|---------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Salesforce                      | Salesforce connector with click-to-call   |
| ServiceNow                      | ServiceNow connector with incident popup  |
| Microsoft 365                   | Outlook calendar sync + Teams coexistence |
| Google Workspace                | Google Calendar sync + web client         |
| SAP                             | SAP connector with activity logging       |
| On-premises Active Directory    | LDAP/AD sync in the admin portal          |

## Practice Exercise

**Scenario**: You have a 30-minute demo scheduled with a mid-market logistics company (600 employees). From your discovery call, you learned:

- They use Microsoft 365 (Outlook, Teams for chat only — no Teams Phone)
- They have an aging Mitel PBX that they want to keep for another 2-3 years
- Their dispatchers use Android phones in the field; office staff use Windows PCs
- Their sales team lives in Salesforce and complains about switching between tools
- The IT Director wants to see the admin portal and deployment options
- The CFO will attend the first 10 minutes and wants to understand the value proposition

**Task**: Plan your 30-minute demo by creating a minute-by-minute agenda:

1. What do you show in the first 10 minutes (while the CFO is present)?
2. What features do you demonstrate in minutes 10-25?
3. How do you close the demo in the final 5 minutes?
4. Which CRM connector do you demonstrate, and why?
5. Which client platforms do you emphasize, and why?

Write your demo plan as a structured agenda with timing, feature, and talking point for each segment.

## Knowledge Check

**Question 1**: What is the maximum number of participants supported in a Rainbow conference?

- A) 50
- B) 80
- C) 120
- D) 250

**Answer**: C — Rainbow supports up to 120 participants in a conference, with up to 49 simultaneous video streams in gallery view. This capacity is included with the Business tier and above at no additional per-meeting charge.

---

**Question 2**: Which Rainbow AI feature automatically generates meeting notes with action items attributed to specific participants?

- A) Live transcription
- B) Smart summaries
- C) Real-time translation
- D) Intelligent routing

**Answer**: B — Smart summaries automatically generate meeting notes after a conference, including key discussion points, decisions made, and action items attributed to specific participants. Live transcription provides real-time speech-to-text during the meeting, which is a related but distinct feature.

---

**Question 3**: How many client platforms does Rainbow support?

- A) 3 (Web, Windows, iOS)
- B) 4 (Web, Windows, macOS, Mobile)
- C) 5 (Web, Windows, macOS, iOS, Android)
- D) 6 (Web, Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, Android TV)

**Answer**: D — Rainbow is available on six platforms: Web (browser), Windows (desktop), macOS (desktop), iOS (mobile), Android (mobile), and Android TV (meeting rooms). This ensures every user type — from executives to field workers to conference rooms — can access Rainbow.

---

**Question 4**: Which CRM connector feature causes a customer's record to appear automatically when they call?

- A) Click-to-call
- B) Presence sync
- C) Screen pop
- D) Call forwarding

**Answer**: C — Screen pop is the feature where the CRM contact record automatically appears on screen when an incoming call is matched to a contact in the CRM. This saves the user from manually searching for the record and ensures they have context before answering.

---

**Question 5**: What should you do BEFORE every sales demo?

- A) Memorize a feature list to recite during the demo
- B) Populate your demo account with realistic data, test your connection, and prepare a backup plan
- C) Send the prospect a 50-page technical whitepaper
- D) Schedule the demo for at least 90 minutes to cover every feature

**Answer**: B — Preparation is critical. A demo account populated with realistic data (contacts, Bubbles, messages), a tested internet connection, and a backup plan (e.g., web client bookmark) ensures a smooth experience. Demos should be tailored and focused, not exhaustive — 30-45 minutes is optimal.

## Lesson Content

### Section 1: The Rainbow Subscription Model

Rainbow uses a per-user, per-month subscription model. There is no large upfront capital expenditure, no multi-year hardware depreciation, and no complex per-feature pricing. Each user is assigned a subscription tier, and the organization is billed monthly (or annually, depending on the contract) based on the total count of users per tier.

The critical insight for Customer Success professionals is that Rainbow supports **mix-and-match** licensing. Unlike platforms that force a single tier across the entire organization, Rainbow lets administrators assign different tiers to different users. This is one of Rainbow's strongest cost optimization levers, and it is a conversation you will have with virtually every customer.

There are five subscription tiers. Each builds on the one below it.

---

### Section 2: Essential (Free Tier)

**Target audience:** Trial users, light communicators, organizations evaluating Rainbow before committing.

**Included features:**

- Instant messaging (1-to-1 and group)
- Peer-to-peer (P2P) audio and video calls
- Presence and availability status
- Bubbles (group collaboration spaces) — create and participate
- File sharing — 1 GB total storage per user
- Basic contact directory
- Web, desktop, and mobile client access

**What is NOT included:**

- No SLA (service level agreement) — Essential is provided as-is
- No PBX integration or softphone capabilities
- No conferencing (only P2P calls)
- No CRM connectors
- No extended file storage
- No admin portal access (company-level management)
- No telephony features (IVR, call routing, dashboards)
- No AI features (transcription, action points)

**Customer Success perspective:**

Essential is not a "cheap option" — it is a strategic entry point. Use it for three purposes:

1. **Trial conversion.** Let prospective customers experience Rainbow's messaging and calling quality before they commit to a paid tier. Essential removes the barrier to entry.
2. **Light user optimization.** In a large organization, many users only need basic messaging. Assign them Essential (free) rather than paying for features they will never use.
3. **Guest and external access.** External collaborators invited into Bubbles can use Essential accounts to participate without requiring the customer to purchase licenses for them.

**Limitation to communicate clearly:** Essential has no SLA. For business-critical communication, customers must use a paid tier.

### Section 3: Business Tier

**Target audience:** Remote and hybrid workers, mobile employees, organizations needing telephony integration.

**Included features — everything in Essential, plus:**

- PBX overlay integration (ALE OXO, OMNI, third-party via CTI/Media Bridge)
- Softphone functionality across all platforms
- Enhanced mobility features (call forwarding, simultaneous ring, voicemail)
- Company directory with advanced search
- Admin portal access for company-level user management
- SLA coverage
- Extended file storage (beyond the 1 GB Essential limit)

**What is NOT included:**

- No multi-party conferencing (still limited to P2P)
- No Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace integration
- No CRM connectors
- No AI features
- No IVR or advanced telephony routing
- No call statistics dashboards

**Customer Success perspective:**

Business is the workhorse tier for organizations that need telephony mobility. The typical Business user is someone who works from home, travels, or moves between office locations and needs their desk phone functionality on their laptop or mobile device. The PBX overlay is the key value — it means the customer does not need to replace their existing telephone system.

**Common scenario:** A company has 500 office-based workers with desk phones connected to an ALE OmniPCX Enterprise. They also have 200 field sales representatives who need to make and receive business calls from their mobile devices. Solution: assign Business tier to the 200 field reps. Their mobile devices become softphones connected to the existing PBX. The 500 office workers can stay on Essential (or get Business if they want softphone on their desktops too).

### Section 4: Enterprise Tier

**Target audience:** Large organizations needing full conferencing, productivity suite integration, and extended collaboration features.

**Included features — everything in Business, plus:**

- Audio/video conferencing — up to 120 audio participants and 49 simultaneous video streams
- Screen sharing and presentation mode in conferences
- Microsoft 365 integration (calendar sync, document collaboration, presence sync)
- Google Workspace integration (calendar sync, document collaboration)
- Extended file storage (significantly larger quotas)
- Meeting recording capabilities
- AI-powered features:
  - Automatic meeting transcription (speech-to-text)
  - Action point extraction from meeting transcripts
  - Meeting bots (automated meeting assistants)

**What is NOT included:**

- No CRM-embedded softphone (that is Rainbow Connect)
- No cloud-native PBX / IVR (that is Rainbow Hub)
- No call statistics dashboards (that is Rainbow Hub)

**Customer Success perspective:**

Enterprise is the tier for organizations that want Rainbow to be their primary collaboration platform — not just telephony, but full meetings, document sharing, and AI-assisted productivity. The conferencing capacity (120 audio, 49 video) handles almost any internal meeting scenario: team standups, department all-hands, board meetings, training sessions.

The Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace integrations are significant because they address the "yet another tool" objection. Calendar events sync with Rainbow, so users see their meetings in one place. Presence status syncs, so colleagues can see availability across both platforms.

The AI features are strong ROI drivers. Meeting transcription saves manual note-taking time. Action point extraction ensures follow-ups are captured automatically. These are features you should demonstrate to customers early in the adoption phase and track usage of in QBRs.

**Common scenario:** A multinational corporation with 5,000 employees needs full collaboration capabilities. They deploy Enterprise for 1,000 managers and knowledge workers who run frequent meetings, Business for 2,500 mobile/remote workers who mainly need telephony, and Essential for 1,500 desk-based workers who only need messaging. Total cost is significantly lower than licensing all 5,000 at the Enterprise tier.

### Section 5: Rainbow Connect

**Target audience:** Sales teams, customer service teams, and any role that works primarily within a CRM system.

**What it is:**

Rainbow Connect is a CRM-embedded softphone. It places Rainbow's calling and communication capabilities directly inside the customer's CRM interface, eliminating the need to switch between applications.

**Supported CRM platforms:**

| CRM | Integration Depth |
|---|---|
| Salesforce | Click-to-call, automatic call logging, screen pop with caller data |
| ServiceNow | ITSM ticket-linked communications, call notes attached to incidents |
| Zendesk | Support ticket-linked softphone, call recording attachment |
| Zoho CRM | Click-to-call, contact sync, call history in CRM |
| Microsoft Dynamics 365 | Click-to-call, activity logging, contact matching |

**Key features:**

- Softphone embedded in the CRM browser tab — no separate Rainbow application needed
- Automatic caller identification and CRM record screen pop
- Call logging directly into CRM records (calls, durations, notes)
- Click-to-call from any phone number field in the CRM
- Presence awareness within the CRM interface

**Customer Success perspective:**

Rainbow Connect is an upsell opportunity within accounts that already have a CRM deployed. The value proposition is straightforward: sales and service teams spend most of their day inside the CRM. Every time they switch to a separate phone or communication app, they lose context and time. Rainbow Connect eliminates that switching cost.

When positioning Rainbow Connect, focus on measurable outcomes:
- Reduced average handle time (AHT) because agents do not switch apps.
- Improved CRM data quality because calls are logged automatically.
- Faster customer response because click-to-call eliminates manual dialing.

**Important note:** Rainbow Connect is typically licensed in addition to (not instead of) a base tier. A user might have a Business + Connect license, or an Enterprise + Connect license. Make sure customers understand this additive model.

### Section 6: Rainbow Hub

**Target audience:** Organizations that need full cloud-native telephony with sovereignty, IVR, and operational dashboards — without any on-premises PBX.

**What it is:**

Rainbow Hub is the most comprehensive tier. It delivers everything in Enterprise plus full cloud telephony features that would traditionally require on-premises PBX hardware.

**Included features — everything in Enterprise, plus:**

- Cloud-native PBX (no on-premises hardware needed)
- IVR (Interactive Voice Response) — automated call routing menus
- Hunt groups and call queues
- Open/close calendars (business hours routing, after-hours handling)
- Call statistics dashboards with real-time and historical analytics
- Full telephony sovereignty — deploy via Rainbow Edge for 100% private infrastructure

**Customer Success perspective:**

Rainbow Hub is the answer for organizations that want to eliminate their on-premises PBX entirely and move to a fully cloud-based telephony model — but with the sovereignty and advanced features that consumer-grade cloud platforms cannot provide.

The IVR, hunt groups, and open/close calendars are capabilities that contact center and customer-facing teams require. The call statistics dashboards give operations managers visibility into call volumes, answer rates, queue times, and agent performance.

Rainbow Hub + Rainbow Edge is the ultimate sovereignty deployment: full cloud telephony with all data staying within the customer's own infrastructure, operated under European law.

**Common scenario:** A French government agency needs cloud telephony but cannot use any U.S.-headquartered cloud service. Solution: Rainbow Hub deployed on Rainbow Edge. They get IVR, call dashboards, conferencing, AI features — all running within their own sovereign infrastructure.

### Section 7: Mix-and-Match Strategy

The mix-and-match strategy is one of the most powerful tools in a Customer Success professional's toolkit. It directly addresses the CFO's cost concerns while ensuring every user has the features they need.

**How it works:**

Within a single Rainbow organization (company account), administrators can assign different subscription tiers to different users. There is no requirement that all users be on the same tier.

**Step-by-step approach for Customer Success:**

1. **Audit the user base.** Work with the customer's IT team to categorize users by their actual communication needs.
2. **Segment into tier groups:**
   - **Essential** — Users who only need messaging and occasional P2P calls. Think receptionists with desk phones already, warehouse workers, part-time staff.
   - **Business** — Users who need softphone and telephony mobility. Think field workers, remote employees, traveling executives.
   - **Enterprise** — Users who run meetings, need conferencing, and benefit from AI features. Think managers, project leads, department heads.
   - **Connect** — Users who live inside a CRM. Think sales reps, account managers, service agents.
   - **Hub** — Users who need cloud-native telephony with IVR and dashboards. Typically contact center agents and operations managers.

3. **Calculate the blended cost.** Show the customer the total monthly cost of the mixed assignment versus the cost of putting everyone on the highest tier they would need.

4. **Review quarterly.** User needs change. A user assigned Essential may now run weekly meetings and need Enterprise. A Business user who joins the sales team may need Connect. Build tier optimization into every QBR.

**Example calculation:**

| User Segment | Count | Tier | Unit Cost (example) | Monthly Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Office-based (messaging only) | 500 | Essential (Free) | EUR 0 | EUR 0 |
| Mobile workers | 300 | Business | EUR 6 | EUR 1,800 |
| Managers / meeting hosts | 150 | Enterprise | EUR 12 | EUR 1,800 |
| Sales team | 50 | Enterprise + Connect | EUR 16 | EUR 800 |
| **Total** | **1,000** | **Mixed** | **Blended: EUR 4.40** | **EUR 4,400** |

If the same organization licensed everyone at Enterprise: 1,000 x EUR 12 = EUR 12,000/month. The mix-and-match approach saves EUR 7,600/month — EUR 91,200/year. That is the kind of number that makes a CFO pay attention.

### Section 8: Subscription Dashboard Navigation

The Rainbow Admin Portal includes a subscription dashboard that provides visibility into the organization's licensing.

**Key dashboard views:**

- **License Summary** — Total licenses purchased per tier, total assigned, total available (unassigned).
- **User Assignment** — Searchable list of all users with their current tier assignment. Filterable by tier, department, site.
- **Usage Analytics** — Per-tier usage data: how many users on each tier are actively using the features included in that tier. This data is essential for identifying optimization opportunities (users paying for Enterprise but only using messaging features should be downgraded).
- **Subscription History** — Timeline of tier changes, additions, and removals.
- **Cost View** — Monthly and annual cost breakdown by tier, department, and site.

**Customer Success actions in the dashboard:**

1. **Identify underutilized licenses.** If 50 Enterprise users have never joined a conference, they might be over-licensed.
2. **Identify upgrade candidates.** If 30 Essential users are consistently hitting the file storage limit or requesting conferencing, they are candidates for a tier upgrade.
3. **Prepare QBR data.** Export license allocation, usage trends, and cost summaries for Quarterly Business Review presentations.
4. **Monitor expansion signals.** A sudden increase in license requests or P2P call volume often precedes the need for conferencing (Enterprise) or telephony (Business/Hub).

## Practice Exercise

**Scenario: License Optimization Workshop**

A mid-size logistics company (800 employees) currently licenses all users on the Enterprise tier at EUR 12/user/month. Their monthly Rainbow bill is EUR 9,600. After reviewing their usage data, you identify the following user segments:

| Segment | Count | Actual Usage Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Warehouse operatives | 250 | Only use messaging for shift updates; never make calls through Rainbow |
| Drivers | 200 | Use softphone on mobile for dispatch calls; never join conferences |
| Office staff | 200 | Use messaging, occasional P2P calls, and file sharing |
| Managers | 100 | Run weekly team meetings (conferencing), use transcription and action points |
| Sales team | 50 | Work entirely in Salesforce; need click-to-call and call logging |

**Tasks:**

1. Propose a tier assignment for each user segment. Justify your choice with specific feature requirements.
2. Calculate the new blended monthly cost and the annual savings compared to the current all-Enterprise model.
3. Identify which user segments represent future upsell opportunities (i.e., users who might need a higher tier as the business evolves).
4. Draft a 3-sentence email to the customer's IT Director proposing this optimization. Make it sound helpful, not like a cost cut.

## Knowledge Check

**Question 1:** Which subscription tier includes PBX overlay and softphone functionality but does NOT include multi-party conferencing?

- A) Essential
- B) Business
- C) Enterprise
- D) Rainbow Hub

**Correct Answer:** B — Business tier includes PBX overlay and softphone for telephony mobility, but conferencing (beyond P2P calls) requires Enterprise or higher.

---

**Question 2:** A customer's sales team works entirely within Salesforce and needs click-to-call with automatic call logging in CRM records. Which Rainbow product should you recommend?

- A) Enterprise tier only
- B) Rainbow Hub
- C) Rainbow Connect (additive to a base tier)
- D) Essential with a third-party plugin

**Correct Answer:** C — Rainbow Connect is a CRM-embedded softphone designed specifically for CRM-centric users. It is licensed as an addition to a base tier (Business or Enterprise).

---

**Question 3:** What is the primary benefit of the mix-and-match licensing strategy?

- A) It allows the organization to use Rainbow for free.
- B) It assigns the right tier to each user based on their actual needs, optimizing total cost.
- C) It eliminates the need for an admin portal.
- D) It converts all users to the Enterprise tier automatically.

**Correct Answer:** B — Mix-and-match lets administrators assign different tiers to different users, ensuring no user is over-licensed (paying for features they do not use) or under-licensed (lacking features they need).

---

**Question 4:** A French hospital needs cloud telephony with IVR, call dashboards, and full data sovereignty (no public cloud dependency). Which combination should you recommend?

- A) Business tier on public cloud
- B) Enterprise tier with Microsoft Teams integration
- C) Rainbow Hub deployed on Rainbow Edge
- D) Essential tier with ANSSI certification

**Correct Answer:** C — Rainbow Hub provides full cloud telephony (IVR, dashboards, call routing), and deploying it on Rainbow Edge ensures all data stays within the hospital's own infrastructure (full sovereignty). The HDS 1.1 certification additionally covers healthcare data hosting requirements in France.

---

**Question 5:** In the subscription dashboard, you notice that 40 Enterprise-tier users have never joined a conference call in 6 months. What action should you take?

- A) Ignore it — they may need conferencing in the future.
- B) Flag these users as candidates for downgrade to Business tier, and discuss with the customer's IT admin during the next QBR.
- C) Automatically downgrade them without informing the customer.
- D) Upgrade them to Rainbow Hub to give them more features.

**Correct Answer:** B — Underutilized Enterprise licenses represent a cost optimization opportunity. The correct approach is to identify the pattern, present it to the customer, and collaboratively decide whether those users should be reassigned to a lower tier. Never make license changes without the customer's agreement.

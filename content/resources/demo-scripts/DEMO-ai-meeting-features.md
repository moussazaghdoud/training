# Demo Script: AI Meeting Features

> **Duration:** 10-15 minutes
> **Audience:** Team leads, department heads, productivity stakeholders, IT decision-makers
> **Prerequisites for Demo Environment:** Rainbow Enterprise account with AI transcription pack enabled, at least 2 participants available for a live conference, microphone and speakers tested, a pre-recorded meeting with transcription and action items available as fallback
> **Last Updated:** February 2026

---

## Pre-Demo Setup Checklist

Before the demo, verify:

- [ ] Rainbow desktop client is logged in with an Enterprise license
- [ ] AI transcription feature is enabled for the account (Admin Portal > Features > AI)
- [ ] A second participant is available (colleague or second device)
- [ ] Microphone and speakers are working (run Rainbow audio test)
- [ ] A pre-existing conference recording with transcription is available as backup
- [ ] Prepare 3-4 talking points to discuss during the live conference (so the transcription has meaningful content)
- [ ] Browser is open for showing the meeting summary afterwards
- [ ] Slide or diagram of the AI feature set is ready for the overview section

---

## Demo Flow

### Opening — The Problem (2 minutes)

**Talking Point:**
"Let me start with a statistic. The average professional spends 23 hours per week in meetings. After those meetings, they spend an additional 4 to 6 hours writing summaries, updating task lists, and sending follow-ups. That is nearly 30 hours a week consumed by meetings and meeting-related administration."

**Talking Point:**
"Rainbow's AI meeting features are designed to eliminate the post-meeting overhead. Real-time transcription, automatic summarization, and action point extraction mean your teams leave every meeting with a complete, searchable record — without anyone taking notes. Let me show you exactly how this works."

---

### Segment 1: Setting Up a Conference (2 minutes)

**[Action: Open Rainbow desktop client]**

**Talking Point:**
"Let me start a conference. In Rainbow, you can start a conference in multiple ways — from a bubble, from a calendar event, or on the fly. I will create one from scratch."

**[Action: Click the "Start Conference" button in the Rainbow client]**

**[Action: Add the second participant by name or email]**

**Talking Point:**
"I have added my colleague to this conference. I could add up to 120 participants on an Enterprise license. The conference supports audio, video, and screen sharing — but today I want to focus specifically on the AI capabilities."

**[Action: Start the conference. Both participants join.]**

**Talking Point:**
"We are now in a live conference. You can see the participant list, the audio controls, and — this is the important part — the AI features panel on the right side."

---

### Segment 2: Activating Real-Time Transcription (2 minutes)

**[Action: Click the "Transcription" toggle in the conference controls]**

**Talking Point:**
"I am now activating live transcription. A notification is sent to all participants that the meeting is being transcribed — this is important for consent and compliance. Every participant sees the notification and can acknowledge it."

**[Action: Show the transcription notification that appears for all participants]**

**Talking Point:**
"Transparency is built in. No one is transcribed without their knowledge. This aligns with GDPR requirements and good corporate governance."

**[Action: Begin speaking clearly about a prepared topic]**

**Sample Script to Speak:**
"Let's discuss the Q3 customer deployment timeline. We need to finalize the pilot group by March 15th. Sarah, can you prepare the site survey for the Lyon office by next Friday? Also, we should schedule a checkpoint meeting with the customer's IT team for the first week of April."

**[Action: Show the transcription panel updating in real time as words are spoken]**

**Talking Point:**
"Watch the transcription panel. It is capturing what we say in real time, attributing each segment to the correct speaker. The speech-to-text engine supports 16 languages and handles accents, technical terminology, and cross-talk."

**Key Moment to Emphasize:**
"Notice the speaker identification. The transcript does not just capture words — it labels who said what. This is critical for accountability. When you review the transcript later, you know exactly who committed to which deliverable."

---

### Segment 3: Demonstrating Accuracy and Features (3 minutes)

**[Action: Continue the simulated meeting discussion]**

**Sample Script to Speak:**
"The budget for the deployment is confirmed at 450,000 euros. We need to ensure the PBX migration happens over a weekend to minimize disruption. Mark, please coordinate with the facilities team to arrange after-hours access for the 22nd and 23rd of March."

**[Action: Point to the live transcript showing numbers, names, and dates captured correctly]**

**Talking Point:**
"Notice that the transcription correctly captured the budget figure — 450,000 euros — the specific dates, and the names. The AI model is trained on business vocabulary and handles numbers, acronyms, and technical terms effectively."

**[Action: Speak in a second language briefly (if multilingual audience)]**

**Talking Point:**
"For multilingual organizations, the transcription supports automatic language detection. If a participant switches to French or German mid-meeting, the engine adapts. This is particularly valuable for pan-European teams."

**[Action: Show the "Highlight" feature — click on a specific line in the transcript to bookmark it]**

**Talking Point:**
"During the meeting, any participant can click on a line in the transcript to highlight it. Think of it as a digital sticky note — it flags that moment as important. These highlights become part of the meeting summary."

**[Action: Open the action items list]**

**Talking Point:**
"Look at the action items. The AI has identified four concrete tasks from our conversation. For each one, it has extracted the responsible person when named, and the deadline when mentioned. These are not guesses — they are directly derived from what was said."

**Key Moment to Emphasize:**
"This is where the productivity gain is transformational. ## Objection Handling

### "Is the transcription accurate enough for business use?"

**Response:** "Rainbow's transcription engine achieves over 95% accuracy in quiet environments with clear speech. For business meetings with standard audio equipment, that accuracy is comparable to a professional human transcriber. The AI also improves over time with domain-specific vocabulary. For critical meetings like board sessions, we recommend reviewing the transcript — but for daily standups and project meetings, the automatic output is production-ready."

### "What about privacy — who has access to the transcripts?"

**Response:** "Transcripts are stored within the Rainbow tenant and are accessible only to meeting participants and designated administrators. They are encrypted at rest with AES-256 and in transit with TLS 1.3. The data is stored in EU data centers and is subject to the same GDPR-compliant data processing agreement as all Rainbow data. With Rainbow Edge, transcripts can be processed and stored entirely on-premises."

### "We already use Otter.ai / Microsoft Copilot / other AI tools."

**Response:** "Third-party transcription tools add another vendor, another data processor, and another security surface to evaluate. Rainbow's AI features are native — they run within the same platform, under the same security certifications, and under the same data processing agreement. There is no additional data sharing with external AI providers. For organizations in regulated industries, this single-platform approach significantly simplifies compliance."

### "Our meetings are in multiple languages — can it handle that?"

**Response:** "Yes. The transcription engine supports 16 languages and can handle language switching within a single meeting. For meetings where participants speak different languages, the transcript captures each segment in the language spoken. Automatic translation of the summary is on the roadmap for later this year."

### "What if participants join by phone — can it still transcribe?"

**Response:** "Yes. The transcription operates on the audio stream, regardless of how participants connect. Phone dial-in participants are transcribed just like app-based participants. Speaker identification for phone participants uses voice profiling rather than account matching, which is slightly less precise but still effective."
